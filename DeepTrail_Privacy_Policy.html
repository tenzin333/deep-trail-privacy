DeepTrail — Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 2026

DeepTrail ("we", "our", or "the extension") is a Chrome browser extension that helps users build a personal knowledge graph from their web browsing. This privacy policy explains what data we collect, how we handle, store, and share it, and how we protect it.

The short version: DeepTrail processes web page content you explicitly save to build your personal knowledge graph. Your data is sent to our backend server for AI processing (embedding and summarization). We share data only with the third-party services listed in Section 5 — solely to provide DeepTrail's functionality. We do not sell, share, or use your data for advertising. You can delete your data at any time.

1. Data We Collect

When you use DeepTrail, we collect the following data:

  • Web page content: Text content extracted from web pages you explicitly choose to save to your knowledge graph. This is used to generate embeddings and summaries. We do not collect content from pages you do not save.
  • Page metadata: The URL, page title, and timestamp of saved pages. This is used for node identification, deduplication, and display in the knowledge graph.
  • Authentication data: If you create an account, we store your email address and an authentication token to identify your account and secure your data.
  • Search queries: Queries you type into DeepTrail's search are sent to our backend to perform semantic search across your knowledge graph.

2. Data We Do NOT Collect

  • We do not collect your general browsing history. We only process pages you explicitly save.
  • We do not collect passwords, form data, financial information, or health information.
  • We do not track your activity across websites.
  • We do not collect data when the extension is inactive.
  • We do not collect any data in the background or without user action.

3. How We Use Your Data

Your data is used exclusively to provide DeepTrail's core functionality:

  • Embedding generation: Page content is sent to the Google Gemini API to generate semantic embeddings (numerical representations) that enable similarity search in your knowledge graph.
  • Summarization: Page content is sent to the Groq API to generate summaries displayed in your knowledge graph nodes.
  • Semantic search: Your search queries are embedded and compared against your stored knowledge graph to find relevant nodes.
  • Knowledge graph storage: Your nodes, edges, and embeddings are stored in our PostgreSQL database to persist your knowledge graph across sessions.

We do not use your data for any other purpose, including training AI models, analytics, profiling, advertising, or any purpose unrelated to providing DeepTrail's knowledge graph functionality.

4. How We Handle Your Data

This section describes how your data is handled at each stage of processing:

  • In transit: All data transmitted between the extension and our backend, and between our backend and third-party APIs, is encrypted using HTTPS/TLS.
  • During processing: Page content is sent to third-party APIs (Gemini, Groq) in real-time for embedding generation and summarization. We do not cache, log, or retain the raw content sent to these APIs beyond what is needed to complete the request. Once the embedding or summary is returned, the raw content is not stored on our backend.
  • At rest: Processed data (embeddings, summaries, metadata) is stored in our PostgreSQL database hosted on Neon.tech. Each user's data is logically isolated — you cannot access another user's knowledge graph.
  • No secondary use: Your data is never used for purposes beyond providing DeepTrail's core knowledge graph features.

6. Chrome Extension Permissions

DeepTrail requests the following browser permissions, each of which is necessary for the extension's core functionality:

  • activeTab: Required to extract text content from the current web page when you explicitly choose to save it to your knowledge graph. This permission is only activated when you interact with the extension — it does not allow background access to your tabs.
  • sidePanel: Required to display DeepTrail's knowledge graph interface in Chrome's side panel.
  • storage: Required to store your authentication token and extension settings locally in your browser using Chrome's chrome.storage API.

DeepTrail does not request or use broad permissions such as tabs, webNavigation, history, or browsingData. We only request the minimum permissions necessary to provide the extension's functionality.

7. Data Storage & Security

  • Your knowledge graph data is stored in a PostgreSQL database hosted on Neon.tech.
  • All data transmission between the extension and our backend uses HTTPS encryption.
  • All data transmission between our backend and third-party APIs (Gemini, Groq) uses HTTPS encryption.
  • Authentication tokens are stored securely using Chrome's chrome.storage API on your local device.
  • Each user's data is logically isolated — you cannot access another user's knowledge graph.
  • We do not store raw page content on our backend after processing is complete. Only embeddings, summaries, and metadata are persisted.

8. Data Retention & Deletion

  • Your data is retained as long as you have an active DeepTrail account.
  • You can delete individual nodes from your knowledge graph at any time through the extension UI.
  • You can delete all nodes at once using the "Clear All" option in the extension UI.
  • You can request complete deletion of all your data (including your account) by contacting us at the email address below. We will delete all your data within 30 days of receiving your request.
  • If you uninstall the extension, your data remains on our server until you request deletion. This is so you can reinstall and recover your knowledge graph if desired.

9. In-Product Disclosures

In addition to this privacy policy, DeepTrail provides the following in-product disclosures:

  • The extension's Chrome Web Store listing clearly describes DeepTrail's data collection and usage practices.
  • DeepTrail only processes page content when you explicitly trigger the save action — no data is collected passively or in the background.
  • Delete actions (individual node deletion and "Clear All") are available directly in the extension UI, giving you immediate control over your stored data.

10. Chrome Web Store Limited Use Compliance

DeepTrail's use of information received from Google APIs adheres to the Chrome Web Store User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements.

Specifically:

  • We limit data use to providing and improving DeepTrail's single purpose: building a personal research knowledge graph.
  • We do not transfer user data to third parties except as described in Section 5, which is necessary to provide DeepTrail's core functionality.
  • We do not use or transfer user data for personalized advertisements.
  • We do not sell user data to data brokers or information resellers.
  • We do not use or transfer user data to determine creditworthiness or for lending purposes.

11. Your Rights

You have the right to:

  • Access your data — your entire knowledge graph is visible in the extension UI.
  • Delete your data — delete individual nodes via the UI, clear all data at once, or request full account deletion by email.
  • Export your data — (planned feature) export your knowledge graph data.
  • Opt out — you can stop using DeepTrail and uninstall the extension at any time. No data is collected after uninstallation.

12. Changes to This Policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time. Changes will be communicated through the Chrome Web Store listing and/or within the extension. Continued use of DeepTrail after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
